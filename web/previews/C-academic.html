About the CHIP

Credit Hour In Pool — a currency indexed to human productivity

The CHIP was first proposed in Got Choices as a complementary currency that could be implemented without a central bank, a large corporation, or a centralized regulatory authority.

One CHIP represents the value produced by one continuously applied hour of adult human work under standardized conditions — unskilled labor, balanced supply and demand, minimal capital equipment.

The MyCHIPs Papers explore a CHIP-based digital currency in detail. The original econometric study, using panel data from 89 countries over 1992–2019, estimated the CHIP value at $2.53 per hour in 2019 dollars.

Adjusted for inflation, today's value is approximately $3.18. This site tracks and publishes ongoing research into CHIP valuation methodology.

Questions & Answers

What is a CHIP?

A CHIP (Credit Hour In Pool) is a proposed unit for measuring value, indexed to one hour of unskilled human labor in a balanced global market.

How is the value calculated?

Using Cobb-Douglas production functions applied to panel data from dozens of countries, estimating the marginal product of unskilled labor.

Can I contribute research?

Yes. Contact us to discuss collaboration. Funding may be available for bona fide research participants working on CHIP valuation.

Where is the source code?

All code and data are open source, hosted at github.com/gotchoices/chip.
